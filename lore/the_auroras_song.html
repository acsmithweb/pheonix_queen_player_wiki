The Final March Of The Warforged

Long ago, before Emberhaven was more than a mere whisper of a village, a night like no other unfolded under the watchful gaze of the Firepeak Mountains. It was a time when the veil between the worlds was thin, and magic flowed as freely as the rivers.

On this fateful night, the sky above the mountains lit up with an aurora, a mesmerizing dance of colors unseen before. But what made this night eternally memorable was not just the visual splendor; the aurora began to sing.

The song was ethereal, a symphony of otherworldly notes that resonated with the soul of the land. It was said that every creature, from the smallest mouse to the tallest tree, listened in rapt attention. The villagers, awed and slightly fearful, gathered to witness this celestial concert.

As the melody swelled, a figure emerged from the heart of the Firepeak Mountains. He was the first Fey King, a being of immense power and majesty. His emergence marked the first true bridge between the fey realm and the mortal world. With hair like moonlight and eyes reflecting the aurora above, walked with a grace that spoke of ancient forests and timeless streams.

The villagers, unsure of his intent, watched as he approached. He spoke not in words but in whispers that rustled like leaves. He spoke of an age of wonder, of harmony between the natural and the mystical, and of the role Emberhaven would play as a guardian of this balance.

As the first light of dawn began to touch the sky, The King turned and walked back towards the mountains. As he left, the aurora faded, and the song slowly dwindled into a haunting echo.

The Night the Aurora Sang became a foundational myth for Emberhaven, shaping its identity and its deep-rooted connection with the fey. The villagers believed that the Fey King's visit blessed the land, making it a nexus of magical energies. This event also instilled a deep respect for the natural world and its mystical inhabitants.

To this day, when the aurora appears, the villagers of Emberhaven look to the Firepeak Mountains, remembering the night their world was touched by fey magic, and they listen, hoping perhaps to once again hear the ancient song of the aurora.
